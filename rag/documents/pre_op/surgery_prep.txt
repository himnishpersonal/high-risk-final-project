# Pre-Surgery Preparation Checklist

## Two Weeks Before Surgery

### Medical Preparation
- Complete all pre-operative medical clearances
- Stop smoking if you smoke (smoking delays healing)
- Avoid anti-inflammatory medications (aspirin, ibuprofen, naproxen) unless approved by surgeon
- Continue all other regular medications unless instructed otherwise
- Inform your surgeon of any new medications or supplements

### Physical Preparation
- Attend pre-operative physical therapy if prescribed
- Practice using crutches before surgery
- Strengthen your upper body and other leg
- Learn the post-operative exercises you'll need to do

### Home Preparation
- Set up a recovery area on the first floor (avoid stairs if possible)
- Stock up on easy-to-prepare meals
- Arrange for help with daily activities for the first few days
- Prepare ice packs and compression wraps

## One Week Before Surgery

### Logistics
- Confirm surgery date and time with hospital
- Arrange transportation to and from hospital
- Confirm you have someone to stay with you for at least 24 hours after surgery
- Fill prescriptions if provided in advance

### Mental Preparation
- Get adequate sleep
- Stay hydrated
- Maintain a healthy diet
- Review post-operative instructions

## The Day Before Surgery

### Fasting Instructions
- **Nothing to eat after midnight** (or as specifically instructed)
- **Clear liquids only** up to 2 hours before surgery (check with your surgical team)
- No chewing gum or candy

### Preparation
- Shower and wash surgical leg thoroughly
- Do not apply lotions, creams, or deodorant on surgery day
- Remove all jewelry, contact lenses, and nail polish
- Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
- Bring a list of all current medications

### Pack for Hospital
- Insurance card and ID
- List of medications and allergies
- Loose-fitting shorts or pants
- Slip-on shoes
- Phone charger

## Day of Surgery

### Before Leaving Home
- Take only approved medications with a small sip of water
- Do not eat or drink anything else
- Brush teeth but do not swallow water
- Arrive at hospital at designated time (usually 2 hours before surgery)

### What to Expect
- Check-in and paperwork
- Pre-operative assessment by nursing staff
- IV placement
- Anesthesia consultation
- Time in pre-op holding area
- Surgery typically takes 1-2 hours
- Recovery room (1-2 hours)
- Discharge home same day (most ACL surgeries are outpatient)

## Common Pre-Surgery Questions

**Q: How long is the recovery?**
A: Return to normal daily activities: 2-3 months. Return to sports: 6-12 months depending on your progress.

**Q: Will I be in a lot of pain?**
A: Pain is manageable with prescribed medications. Most patients describe it as moderate discomfort, not severe pain.

**Q: Can I drive after surgery?**
A: Not for several weeks, especially if surgery is on your right leg or you're taking narcotic pain medication.

**Q: When can I shower?**
A: Usually 48-72 hours after surgery, or as directed by your surgeon. Keep dressing dry.

**Q: What if I get nervous?**
A: This is completely normal. Write down your questions and call your surgeon's office. They're here to help.

## Emergency Contact

If you develop any illness (cold, flu, fever, infection) in the days before surgery, contact your surgeon immediately. Surgery may need to be postponed to ensure best outcomes.

## Your Surgical Team is Here for You

Don't hesitate to reach out with any questions or concerns before your surgery. There are no "silly" questions - we want you to feel prepared and confident.
