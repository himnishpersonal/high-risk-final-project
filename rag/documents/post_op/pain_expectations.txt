# Post-Operative Pain Management Guidelines

## Normal Pain Expectations After ACL Surgery

### Days 1-3: Acute Phase
- Moderate to severe pain is normal (4-7 on pain scale)
- Peak pain usually occurs within first 48 hours
- Pain should be manageable with prescribed medications
- Ice application every 2-3 hours helps reduce pain and swelling

### Days 4-7: Early Recovery
- Pain should begin decreasing
- Typical pain range: 3-5 on pain scale
- You may start reducing pain medication
- Some discomfort during physical therapy is expected

### Days 8-14: Progressive Improvement
- Pain should be mild (1-3 on pain scale)
- Most patients transition to over-the-counter pain relief
- Pain during exercises is normal but should not be sharp
- Rest pain should be minimal

## When to Contact Your Care Team

**Severe pain (8-10) that:**
- Does not improve with medication
- Prevents you from sleeping
- Gets worse instead of better after day 3
- Is accompanied by fever or unusual swelling

## Pain Management Tips

1. **Stay ahead of the pain**: Take medications as prescribed, not just when pain is severe
2. **Ice therapy**: Apply ice for 20 minutes every 2-3 hours for the first week
3. **Elevation**: Keep leg elevated above heart level when resting
4. **Gentle movement**: Some movement helps - complete immobility can increase stiffness and pain
5. **Sleep positioning**: Use pillows to support and elevate your leg

## Pain vs Warning Signs

**Normal post-op pain:**
- Dull, aching sensation around the surgical site
- Increases with activity, decreases with rest
- Gradually improves day by day
- Responds to ice and medication

**Warning signs (contact your surgeon):**
- Sharp, stabbing pain that suddenly worsens
- Pain in the calf (may indicate blood clot)
- Pain with fever over 101°F
- Pain with redness spreading from incision
- Pain that prevents you from moving your leg at all
