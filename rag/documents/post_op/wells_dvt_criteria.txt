# Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) Prevention and Recognition

## What is DVT?

Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) is a blood clot that forms in a deep vein, usually in the leg. It is a rare but serious complication after ACL surgery that requires immediate medical attention.

## Risk Factors After ACL Surgery

- Reduced mobility in the first few weeks
- Leg immobilization or extended sitting
- Surgery itself increases clotting risk
- Dehydration
- History of blood clots

## Warning Signs of DVT

**Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:**

### Calf Symptoms
- Pain or tenderness in the calf, especially when standing or walking
- Swelling in one leg (especially if only one leg is affected)
- Warmth in the affected area
- Red or discolored skin on the leg

### Chest Symptoms (Pulmonary Embolism - Medical Emergency)
- Sudden shortness of breath
- Chest pain that worsens with deep breathing
- Rapid heart rate
- Coughing up blood

**Call 911 immediately if you have chest symptoms**

## Wells Score for DVT Assessment

Your care team may use this scoring system to assess DVT risk:

**Major criteria (1 point each):**
- Active cancer
- Paralysis or recent immobilization
- Recently bedridden for >3 days or major surgery within 12 weeks
- Localized tenderness in deep vein distribution
- Entire leg swelling
- Calf swelling >3cm compared to other leg
- Pitting edema in symptomatic leg
- Collateral superficial veins

**If Wells score is high, immediate ultrasound evaluation is recommended**

## Prevention Strategies

1. **Early mobility**: Begin gentle ankle pumps and leg exercises immediately after surgery
2. **Hydration**: Drink plenty of water (8-10 glasses per day)
3. **Compression**: Wear compression stockings as prescribed
4. **Movement**: Avoid prolonged sitting or lying in one position
5. **Blood thinners**: Take any prescribed anticoagulants exactly as directed

## Exercises to Prevent DVT

### Ankle Pumps (Do these every hour while awake)
- Point your toes down, then pull them up toward your shin
- Repeat 10-15 times per session
- This activates calf muscles and promotes blood flow

### Ankle Circles
- Rotate your ankle clockwise 10 times
- Rotate counterclockwise 10 times
- Helps prevent blood pooling in lower leg

## When to Call Your Doctor

**Same-day evaluation needed for:**
- New calf pain with swelling
- One leg noticeably larger than the other
- Unusual warmth in calf or lower leg
- Skin color changes in the leg

**Emergency department for:**
- Any breathing difficulty
- Chest pain
- Rapid heartbeat with shortness of breath

## Timeline

DVT most commonly occurs within the first 2 weeks after surgery, but can develop up to 90 days post-operatively. Stay vigilant even as you recover.
